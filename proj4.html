HAND-E Robot

About The project

Hand-E is a mobile manipulator robot equipped with a 6-degree-of-freedom (DOF) joint manipulator. The primary objective of this project is to develop the forward and inverse kinematics for a 6 DOF robot. For forward kinematics, the Denavit-Hartenberg (DH) parameters were employed to determine the transformation matrix, and for inverse kinematics, the Inverse Jacobian matrix was used to calculate the robot's joint angles based on the position of the end effector. The robot is designed to operate in areas where human reachability is challenging, performing repetitive tasks such as nailing, screwing, welding, bolting, sawing, drilling, measuring, marking, and other tool-related operations. Its ability to navigate autonomously allows it to efficiently handle these tasks in environments that are difficult or unsafe for humans.
